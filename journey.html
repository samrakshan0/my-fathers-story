My Father's Medical Journey

Pain in Lower Leg

The journey began with my father experiencing persistent and severe pain in his lower leg. This discomfort was not only physically debilitating but also marked the onset of a series of complex medical challenges that would follow.

Cauda Equina Syndrome

Following the initial leg pain, my father was diagnosed with Cauda Equina Syndrome (CES). This rare condition involves the compression of nerve roots in the lumbar spine, leading to symptoms such as severe lower back pain, sciatica, saddle anesthesia, and bladder and bowel dysfunction. CES is considered a medical emergency requiring prompt surgical intervention to prevent permanent neurological damage. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Paralysis in Lower Body

As the condition progressed, my father experienced paralysis in his lower body. This loss of motor function significantly impacted his mobility and independence, necessitating comprehensive medical care and support.

Discectomy L5S1 Surgery

To address the nerve compression causing his symptoms, my father underwent a discectomy at the L5-S1 level. This surgical procedure involves removing a portion of a herniated disc to relieve pressure on spinal nerves, aiming to alleviate pain and restore function.

Bone Tuberculosis Found in Biopsy

Subsequent medical evaluations, including a biopsy, revealed the presence of bone tuberculosis (TB). This infectious disease affects the bones and can lead to severe complications if not treated promptly. The diagnosis added complexity to his treatment regimen.

COVID-19 Struggle

During the global COVID-19 pandemic, my father contracted the virus. Battling COVID-19 posed additional risks and challenges, especially given his existing health conditions, and required careful medical management.

Left Leg Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)

He developed Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) in his left leg, a condition where blood clots form in deep veins. DVT can lead to serious complications, including pulmonary embolism, if not treated effectively. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Neurogenic Bowel and Bladder

My father experienced neurogenic bowel and bladder dysfunction, conditions resulting from nerve damage that affect the normal function of the bowel and bladder. This led to challenges in managing excretory functions, requiring specialized care and interventions.

High-Grade Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma Diagnosis

Approximately a month later, he was diagnosed with high-grade Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma, an aggressive form of lymphatic cancer. This diagnosis necessitated immediate and intensive treatment to combat the rapidly progressing disease.

6 Cycles of R-CHOP Chemotherapy

To treat the lymphoma, my father underwent six cycles of R-CHOP chemotherapy, a combination regimen including Rituximab, Cyclophosphamide, Doxorubicin, Vincristine, and Prednisone. This treatment aimed to eradicate cancerous cells and achieve remission.

1 Year of Physiotherapy

Following chemotherapy, he engaged in a year-long physiotherapy program designed to restore strength, mobility, and function. The rehabilitation process was crucial in improving his quality of life and aiding recovery from the physical toll of his illnesses and treatments.

Relapse of Disease

Despite previous treatments, the disease relapsed, indicating the persistence of cancerous cells. This setback required reevaluation of his treatment plan and consideration of alternative therapeutic strategies to manage the recurrence.

Journey to India for Treatment

In pursuit of advanced medical care, my father traveled to India, a country renowned for its medical expertise and facilities. This journey was undertaken with the hope of accessing specialized treatments not readily available elsewhere, marking a significant step in his ongoing battle against the disease.

2 Cycles of RGDP Chemotherapy

While in India, he underwent two cycles of RGDP chemotherapy, a regimen comprising Rituximab, Gemcitabine, Dexamethasone, and Cisplatin. This combination aimed to target and eliminate the resilient cancer cells that had persisted despite previous treatments.

Stem Cell Bone Marrow Transplant

Following chemotherapy, my father received a stem cell bone marrow transplant. This procedure involved replacing damaged bone marrow with healthy stem cells to restore normal blood cell production, offering a potential pathway to remission.

Second Relapse After 2.5 Months

Despite the aggressive treatments, the disease relapsed again just two and a half months later. This recurrence underscored the aggressive nature of his condition and the challenges in achieving sustained remission.

Journey to China for CAR T-Cell Therapy

Seeking innovative treatment options, my father traveled to China to undergo CAR T-cell therapy. This cutting-edge immunotherapy involves modifying a patient's own T-cells to recognize and attack cancer cells, offering hope for those with refractory cancers.

Treatment with Zanubrutinib (Brukinsa)

After CAR T-cell therapy, he was prescribed Zanubrutinib, commercially known as Brukinsa. This targeted therapy is a Bruton's tyrosine kinase (BTK) inhibitor designed to interfere with cancer cell growth and survival pathways.

Relapse After 6 Months

Six months later, the disease relapsed once more, indicating resistance to the previous treatments and necessitating the exploration of further therapeutic options.

Plan for Allogeneic CAR T-Cell Therapy in China

In response to the latest relapse, plans were made to return to China for allogeneic CAR T-cell therapy. Unlike autologous therapy, this approach uses T-cells from a healthy donor, potentially offering a more robust immune response against the cancer.

2 Cycles of Polatuzumab Combined with Chemotherapy

Prior to the planned therapy, he underwent two cycles of Polatuzumab combined with chemotherapy. Polatuzumab vedotin is an antibody-drug conjugate targeting CD79b, delivering cytotoxic agents directly to cancerous B-cells.

Allogeneic Bone Marrow Transplant

Finally, my father underwent an allogeneic bone marrow transplant, receiving healthy stem cells from a compatible donor. This procedure aimed to reconstitute his bone marrow with cancer-free cells, offering a chance for long-term remission.
